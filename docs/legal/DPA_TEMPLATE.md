## 2. Order Of Precedence

If this DPA conflicts with the main agreement, this DPA controls only for
personal-data processing and data-protection matters. Commercial terms,
liability caps, fees, suspension, and governing law remain controlled by the
main agreement unless this DPA expressly says otherwise.

## 3. Definitions

Unless this DPA defines them differently, the terms "controller", "processor",
"subprocessor", "personal data", "personal data breach", "processing", "data
subject", and "supervisory authority" have the meanings given to them in the
GDPR and UK GDPR.

For this DPA:

- "Applicable Data Protection Law" means the GDPR, UK GDPR, Data Protection Act
  2018, Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations where applicable, and
  any other data-protection or privacy law that applies to the processing.
- "Controller personal data" means personal data processed by ComplyEur on
  behalf of Controller under the main agreement.
- "Customer data" means data submitted to, stored in, generated by, or exported
  from the ComplyEur service by or for Controller.
- "Restricted transfer" means a transfer of personal data that requires a
  safeguard under Applicable Data Protection Law because the destination is not
  covered by an adequacy decision or equivalent lawful transfer basis.
- "Services" means the ComplyEur software, support, billing, security,
  operational, and related services provided under the main agreement.

## 4. Subject Matter And Duration

- Subject matter: processing customer-managed employee and travel compliance
  data to provide the ComplyEur service.
- Duration: for the term of the main agreement and until return, deletion, or
  retention obligations are completed.
- Processing frequency: continuous while the customer uses the service.

## 5. Nature And Purpose Of Processing

ComplyEur processes personal data to:

- host customer accounts and workspaces;
- store employee, trip, alert, import, export, and audit records;
- calculate Schengen 90/180-day compliance status;
- provide alerts, reports, exports, audit logs, retention, and support
  operations;
- protect the service through authentication, authorization, rate limiting,
  monitoring, logging, and incident response;
- administer billing and subscription entitlement where applicable.

## 6. Categories Of Data Subjects

- Customer administrators and users.
- Customer-managed employees, contractors, or travellers whose travel is tracked
  by the customer.
- Billing, support, and privacy-request contacts.
- Website visitors and users where consent or operational cookies are used.

## 7. Categories Of Personal Data

- Account data: name, email address, company details, role, authentication
  identifiers, MFA state, session metadata, and preferences.
- Employee data: name, optional email, nationality or nationality type where
  provided, employment/customer reference fields where provided.
- Trip data: entry and exit dates, countries, trip purpose, job reference,
  private-trip flag, and travel-day totals.
- Compliance data: rolling-window calculations, risk states, alert messages,
  acknowledgements, exports, and reports.
- Import data: uploaded row values, validation errors, result summaries, and
  temporary parsed payloads.
- Billing data: customer, subscription, invoice, tax, and payment metadata;
  full payment card data is handled by Stripe.
- Operational metadata: IP-derived request metadata, timestamps, audit logs,
  security logs, rate-limit keys, error context, consent preferences, and support
  messages.

ComplyEur does not intentionally require special-category data, children's data,
criminal-offence data, passport scans, visa documents, or full payment card
details for the core service.

## 8. Controller Obligations

Controller will:

- provide lawful, documented processing instructions;
- ensure it has a lawful basis for collecting and submitting personal data to
  ComplyEur;
- ensure customer users are authorized to access the workspace and submit data;
- provide accurate data and keep it up to date;
- avoid submitting prohibited or unnecessary special-category data unless a
  separate written agreement permits it;
- respond to data-subject requests where Controller is responsible for the
  underlying employee/traveller data.

## 9. Processor Obligations

Processor will:

- process personal data only on documented Controller instructions, including
  the main agreement, this DPA, customer configuration, and customer use of the
  service;
- ensure personnel with access to personal data are subject to confidentiality
  obligations and are given access only where needed to provide the service or
  comply with applicable law;
- implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures;
- assist Controller with data-subject requests where technically feasible;
- assist with GDPR/UK GDPR Articles 32-36 obligations where applicable and
  reasonable;
- make available information reasonably needed to demonstrate compliance with
  this DPA;
- notify Controller if Processor believes an instruction infringes applicable
  data-protection law, unless prohibited by law.

Processor will take reasonable steps to ensure the reliability of employees,
contractors, agents, and subprocessors who may access Controller personal data.

## 10. Security Measures

ComplyEur's current technical and organizational measures include:

- TLS for data in transit.
- Provider-backed encryption at rest for hosted database, storage, and platform
  services.
- Supabase Row Level Security for tenant isolation.
- Role-based access controls and least-privilege permission checks.
- MFA controls for privileged actions where implemented.
- Service-role key isolation to server-side allowlisted code paths.
- Centralized request origin checks for mutating routes.
- Import validation, file-size limits, and macro-enabled spreadsheet rejection.
- Audit logging for GDPR and administrative events.
- Error monitoring with redaction and without intentionally sending raw employee
  travel data.
- Consent-gated analytics and non-essential cookies.
- Incident response process in `docs/INCIDENT_RESPONSE.md`.
- Backup, restore, and recovery procedures in `docs/RUNBOOK.md`, subject to the
  current release evidence and risk acceptances.

Security evidence is tracked in `docs/legal/PRIVACY_OPERATING_RECORD.md` and
`docs/security/MINIMUM_SECURITY_BAR_PROGRESS.md`.

## 11. Subprocessors

ComplyEur may use subprocessors to provide the service. The current operational
register is maintained in `docs/legal/PRIVACY_OPERATING_RECORD.md` and the DPA
evidence/signoff tracker is maintained in `docs/legal/DPA_READINESS.md`.

Current expected subprocessors:

- Supabase: database, authentication, storage, and related infrastructure.
- Vercel: application hosting, deployment, edge delivery, and runtime logs.
- Stripe: billing, subscriptions, invoices, and payment processing.
- Resend: transactional email delivery.
- Sentry: error monitoring and reliability diagnostics.
- Google Analytics: consent-gated analytics.
- CookieYes: consent management.
- Upstash Redis: rate limiting and abuse prevention, where enabled.
- Cloudflare Turnstile: bot protection, where enabled.
- Email/support tooling used for customer support and privacy request handling.

Processor will keep the register current and notify Controller of material
subprocessor changes where contractually required.

Subprocessor notice and objection terms must be confirmed in the main agreement
or final DPA approval. If the main agreement is silent, legal review should
decide the notice period before this DPA is sent as final.

## 12. International Transfers

Personal data may be processed in the UK, EEA, and other locations where
ComplyEur or approved subprocessors operate. Where a restricted transfer occurs,
the parties will rely on appropriate safeguards such as an adequacy decision,
the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum
or IDTA, the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and UK Extension where applicable,
or equivalent lawful transfer mechanism.

ComplyEur must not make public claims of exclusive UK/EEA processing unless
current provider and dashboard evidence supports that claim.

## 13. Incident And Breach Notification

Processor will notify Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a
personal data breach involving Controller personal data.

Where known and lawful to disclose, the notification will include:

- nature of the breach;
- affected data categories and approximate data-subject count;
- likely consequences;
- containment and mitigation actions;
- contact point for follow-up;
- updates as investigation proceeds.

Controller remains responsible for supervisory-authority and data-subject
notifications unless applicable law requires Processor to act directly.

## 14. Data Subject Rights And Assistance

Controller remains responsible for validating and responding to requests from
customer-managed employees/travellers. Processor will provide reasonable
technical assistance through the service and privacy-support process for access,
rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, and portability requests.

Customer administrators can use the in-app GDPR tools for employee-linked DSAR,
anonymisation, soft-delete, and hard-delete workflows where available.
Account-level, billing, auth, support, monitoring, and processor-backed requests
may require manual handling as described in
`docs/legal/PRIVACY_OPERATING_RECORD.md`.

If Processor receives a data-subject request relating to Controller personal
data, Processor will notify Controller where legally permitted. Processor will
not respond to that request except on Controller's documented instruction or
where required by law; where legally permitted, Processor will inform Controller
before responding directly.

## 15. DPIA And Prior Consultation Assistance

Taking into account the nature of the processing and information available to
Processor, Processor will provide reasonable assistance with data protection
impact assessments and supervisory-authority consultations that Controller
reasonably determines are required under Applicable Data Protection Law for
Controller personal data processed through the service.

## 16. Return And Deletion

On termination or written Controller instruction, Processor will delete or return
personal data according to the main agreement, this DPA, product functionality,
and applicable law.

Deletion may be limited where retention is required for legal, tax, accounting,
security, fraud-prevention, dispute, or compliance purposes. Backup copies are
restore-only operational copies and expire according to the relevant provider
backup window; selective deletion is not applied inside existing backups.

Where Controller requests written confirmation of deletion, Processor will
provide reasonable confirmation after deletion is completed, subject to legal
retention, backup expiry, security, and other documented limitations.

## 17. Audit And Compliance Information

Processor will provide reasonable compliance documentation on request, including
security summaries, subprocessors, and data-protection records where available.
Additional audits require reasonable notice, must be scoped to Controller
personal data, and must not compromise the security, confidentiality, or
availability of other customers or the service.

## 18. Confidentiality And Notices

Each party will protect non-public information received from the other party in
connection with this DPA and will use it only as needed to perform or enforce the
main agreement, comply with law, or protect the service and affected parties.

Notices under this DPA must be sent in writing using the notice addresses,
account owner email, privacy contact, or other notice mechanism specified in the
main agreement or customer account. The final customer package must identify the
customer privacy/security notice contact and ComplyEur privacy notice contact.

## 19. Liability And Governing Terms

Liability, indemnities, exclusions, governing law, jurisdiction, payment,
suspension, and termination terms follow the main agreement unless this DPA
expressly states otherwise.
